# Welcome — Getting Started

Hi. You're about to start something that most people your age haven't done: spend four months building real, working expertise with AI — not just messing around with it, but learning to use it properly, in a professional context, to do things that actually matter.

By the end of this, you'll have a GitHub page you can put on your CV or university application. When someone asks "do you have any experience with AI?" — and they will ask, increasingly — you'll have something to show them.

This document tells you how to get started.

---

## What you're building

Over four months you'll:

1. **Learn UK contract law** — not to become a lawyer, but because it's a fascinating subject and it gives us something real and complex to work with
2. **Get good at using Claude** — specifically at asking precise questions, giving useful context, and knowing when to push back
3. **Build a portfolio** — a GitHub repository and public website that documents your learning journey, honest and specific

The point is not to have all the answers. The point is to show how you think.

---

## What is Claude Code?

Claude Code is an AI assistant made by Anthropic. Unlike a search engine, it holds a conversation — you build on previous messages, ask follow-up questions, and have it explain things multiple ways until they make sense.

Think of it less like Google and more like a very well-read study partner who's available whenever you are.

**Important:** Claude can be wrong. It can be confidently wrong. Part of what you're learning is how to tell the difference between Claude being helpful and Claude making things up. This is one of the most valuable skills you'll develop.

**Also important:** Claude's capabilities are not fixed. Depending on how it's set up, it may or may not be able to search the web, access recent information, or use external tools. One of the AI skills you'll build is knowing how to find out what your AI can actually do — and not assuming it either can or can't do something without checking.

## Setting up GitHub Pages

Your repo is already on GitHub. To make it a public-facing site:

1. Go to your repo on GitHub
2. Click **Settings** → **Pages**
3. Under "Source", select **main branch** and the **/ (root)** folder
4. Click Save — your site will be live at `https://[your-username].github.io/[repo-name]`

## How each session works

Each week you'll spend about an hour with Claude. Every session follows roughly the same shape:

1. **Opening question** — your coach gives you something to think about before Claude enters the picture
2. **Exploration** — you work through the week's topic with Claude, following your curiosity
3. **Journal entry** — at the end, you write a reflection (copy `journal/template.md`, rename it `journal/week-XX.md`)

The journal entries are the backbone of your portfolio. They don't need to be polished essays — they need to be **honest**. What confused you? What surprised you? What do you still not understand? Those are more interesting than a clean summary of facts.

---

## A few ground rules

**Go slow to go fast.** When Claude gives you an answer, don't just accept it and move on. Ask "why?" Ask for an example. Ask what the counterargument is.

**Write in your own voice.** Your journal entries should sound like you. If they sound like Claude wrote them, something's gone wrong.

**When in doubt, push back.** If Claude says something that seems off, say so. "I'm not sure that's right — can you explain your reasoning?" is one of the most useful things you can say to an AI.

**Keep going even when it's frustrating.** Some sessions will feel slow. Some prompts won't work first time. That's learning.
