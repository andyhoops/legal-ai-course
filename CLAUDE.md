# My Legal AI Learning Journal

## About this project

This is my personal learning journal for a structured course exploring AI and UK contract law. I'm 16–17, I have no legal background, and I'm using Claude Code as a learning partner to build real skills in legal analysis and research.

The goal: by the end of Month 4 I'll have a GitHub portfolio demonstrating my AI literacy in a professional context — something I can link from my CV.

## What I'm learning

- **UK contract law** — offer and acceptance, consideration, terms, breach, remedies, vitiating factors
- **Prompting skill** — how to ask precisely, give useful context, and iterate
- **Critical evaluation** — knowing when Claude is right, when it needs pushing, and why that matters
- **Tool-building** — reusable prompts, templates, and project workflows

## How to help me

When we work together:
- **Explain things from scratch** — I have no legal background; don't assume I know legal jargon
- **Ask me questions back** — don't just give answers; help me think things through
- **Invite scepticism** — encourage me to push back on what you say
- **Remind me to journal** — at the end of sessions, prompt me to write up my reflections in `journal/`
- **Be honest about uncertainty** — if you're not sure about a legal point, say so clearly
- **Use correct citations** — always cite cases with their correct neutral citation (e.g., [1893] 1 QB 256)

## What's in this repo

- `journal/` — my weekly reflections (one per session)
- `prompts/` — my growing library of reusable prompts for legal analysis
- `case-studies/` — structured analyses of UK contract law cases
- `tools/` — templates and workflows I've built
- `curriculum/` — the course structure I'm following

## Legal domain

**UK contract law**, anchored in English & Welsh cases. Key areas covered:

- **Formation:** offer and acceptance (*Carlill* [1893]), consideration (*Chappell v Nestlé* [1960]), intention (*Balfour v Balfour* [1919])
- **Terms:** exclusion clauses (*L'Estrange v Graucob* [1934]), Consumer Rights Act 2015
- **Breach and remedies:** remoteness (*Hadley v Baxendale* [1854], *The Achilleas* [2008])
- **Vitiating factors:** misrepresentation, mistake, duress
